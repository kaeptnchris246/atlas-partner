1 · Basics

What "trading" really is

Trading means buying an asset (e.g. crypto) at one price and selling at another — the profit is the difference. But prices move up and down constantly, so there's both gain and loss. Nobody wins every time; the goal is to win more than you lose over time, while controlling risk.

2 · Glossary

Key terms you'll hear

ExchangeThe marketplace where you trade (we use Binance).
OrderYour instruction to buy or sell.
SpreadThe gap between buy and sell price.
VolatilityHow much the price swings — high = risky.
Stop-LossAn auto-sell that limits your loss.
LeverageBorrowed size — multiplies gains AND losses. Avoid as a beginner.
GridA bot strategy: many orders in a range, profiting from swings.
DCABuying in small steps to average your cost.
3 · The heart of it

Risk management before profit

Pros don't win every trade — they manage risk well. The core rules: never put everything into one trade; risk only a small share per trade (e.g. 1–2% of your capital); always set a stop-loss; and never trade money you need for living. Protecting your capital is more important than chasing a big win.

Watch out

Common beginner mistakes

FOMOChasing a coin because it's pumping — usually too late.
Revenge tradingTrying to win back a loss with a bigger, emotional bet.
No stop-lossLetting a small loss grow into a big one.
Too much leverageMultiplying risk until one move wipes you out.
Golden rules

Do & Don't

DO

  • Only spare money
  • Always set stop-loss
  • Practice first
  • Keep learning

DON'T

  • Never borrow to trade
  • No hype / FOMO
  • Don't trade on emotion
  • No "guaranteed profit"
